Top Threats in Australia

  • 1. Business Email Compromise (BEC): Remains a high-impact threat causing significant financial loss. Attackers use sophisticated social engineering to impersonate executives or suppliers to divert payments.

    Source: ACSC Annual Cyber Threat Report, CyberCX

  • 2. Ransomware & Extortion: A persistent threat to businesses of all sizes, with attackers increasingly targeting critical infrastructure and supply chains, causing major operational disruption.

    Source: CyberCX Cyber Security Review

  • 3. Identity & Credential Theft: Phishing and other methods are used to steal user credentials, which are then leveraged to gain initial access for broader attacks against corporate networks.

  • 1. Phishing & Scams: The most prevalent threat category, leading to significant direct financial losses for individuals and businesses through fraudulent requests and credential harvesting.

    Source: CERT NZ, Kordia Security Reports

  • 2. Ransomware: Continues to be a major disruptive force, with attacks causing significant downtime and financial cost for New Zealand organisations.

    Source: Kordia Security Reports

  • 3. Insecure Software & Systems: The exploitation of unpatched vulnerabilities and misconfigured cloud services remains a primary vector for attackers to gain initial access.

    Source: NCSC Threat Reports

Security Information & Event Management (SIEM)

The foundational layer that centralizes log collection, correlation, and alerting. It provides the essential visibility and audit trail for investigations and is critical for delivering compliance-as-a-service offerings.

Profitability Driver: Essential for offering high-value compliance services and demonstrating security posture to auditors.

Security Orchestration, Automation, & Response (SOAR)

The automation engine of the SOC. SOAR integrates disparate security tools and automates incident response workflows (playbooks), drastically reducing Mean Time to Respond (MTTR) by executing manual tasks at machine speed.

Profitability Driver: The single biggest driver of operational efficiency. Allows a single analyst to manage more clients, directly boosting profit margins.

Extended Detection & Response (XDR)

Unifies and correlates data from endpoints, network, cloud, and identity into a single platform. It provides a holistic view of an attack, speeding up investigations and improving detection accuracy.

Profitability Driver: Reduces tool sprawl and licensing costs while improving analyst efficiency by eliminating "swivel-chair" analysis between multiple consoles.

Identity Security

Focuses on securing user and machine identities to prevent unauthorized access. This includes Identity & Access Management (IAM), Privileged Access Management (PAM), and Identity Threat Detection & Response (ITDR) to combat credential theft and abuse—the top initial access vector.

Profitability Driver: Directly addresses the most common attack path. Offering Identity Security services prevents costly breaches and justifies premium service tiers.

User & Entity Behavior Analytics (UEBA)

Uses machine learning to baseline normal user and device activity, then detects anomalous behaviors that could indicate insider threats or compromised accounts that evade traditional signature-based tools.

Profitability Driver: Enables the offering of advanced, high-margin threat detection services focused on sophisticated, hard-to-detect threats.

Threat Intelligence Platform (TIP)

Aggregates and operationalizes threat intelligence (IoCs, TTPs) from multiple sources. This data enriches alerts with context, enabling faster triage and informing proactive threat hunting exercises.

Profitability Driver: Underpins premium service offerings like dedicated threat intelligence feeds and proactive threat hunting retainers.

Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM)

An evolution of vulnerability management. CTEM is a continuous five-stage program to scope, discover, prioritize, validate, and mobilize responses to threats against the enterprise attack surface. It provides a proactive approach to reducing risk.

Profitability Driver: Moves the service from reactive scanning to a proactive, high-value strategic program, justifying a recurring revenue retainer model.

Australia: The Essential Eight

The ACSC's Essential Eight is the de facto standard for cybersecurity maturity in Australia. MSSPs must provide services that directly map to implementing, managing, and attesting to these eight baseline controls, forming the core of a robust compliance offering.

New Zealand: Critical Controls

The NCSC's minimum standards and critical controls (based on frameworks like the CIS Top 18) serve as the benchmark for government and critical infrastructure. MSSP services should be aligned to help clients meet these public sector supply chain requirements.

Strategic Roadmap & Future Outlook

A blueprint for building a competitive, profitable MSSP in the ANZ region, and the key trends that will shape the market's future.

Blueprint for Success

  1. Lead with Automation-First: Build your SOC on a converged platform with integrated SOAR and AI to maximize efficiency and scalability.
  2. Monetize the Compliance Burden: Market premium "Compliance-as-a-Service" offerings for SOCI Act, NDB, and Essential Eight.
  3. Adopt Value-Based Pricing: Structure tiers around outcomes like guaranteed MTTR to justify premium pricing.
  4. Target High-Risk Verticals: Focus on critical infrastructure, healthcare, and financial services.
  5. Champion the Co-Managed Model: Position as a strategic partner to the client's CISO, handling tactical operations while they retain strategic oversight.

Evolving Opportunities

  • Shift to Proactive Security: Growing demand for services like threat hunting and Continuous Threat Exposure Management (CTEM).
  • Convergence of IT & Security: SMEs seek a single partner for both IT management and security.
  • Sovereign Cloud & Data Residency: A key differentiator for government and critical infrastructure clients.
  • The Next Frontier: OT Security: Securing industrial control systems is a nascent, high-value market.
